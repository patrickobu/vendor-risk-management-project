# Contract Security Requirements

**Organisation:** SecureSupply Corp
**Document:** Minimum Security Requirements for Vendor Contracts
**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 2025

---

## Purpose

Every vendor contract at SecureSupply Corp must include security
clauses appropriate to the vendor's tier. This document defines
the minimum security requirements that must appear in every
contract before it is signed.

---

## Requirements Applicable to ALL Vendors (Tiers 1, 2, and 3)

These clauses must appear in every vendor contract regardless
of tier:

**1. Compliance with laws**
The vendor must comply with all applicable laws and regulations
relating to data protection, privacy, and information security
in the jurisdictions where they operate.

**2. Incident notification — basic**
The vendor must notify SecureSupply Corp of any security incident
that could affect SecureSupply's data or systems within 72 hours
of discovery.

**3. Confidentiality**
The vendor must keep all SecureSupply information confidential
and must not disclose it to third parties without written consent.

**4. Contract termination — data return**
Upon termination of the contract the vendor must return or
securely destroy all SecureSupply data within 30 days and
provide written confirmation that destruction has occurred.

---

## Additional Requirements for Tier 2 Vendors

In addition to the above, all Tier 2 vendor contracts must include:

**5. Security standards compliance**
The vendor must maintain security controls equivalent to ISO 27001
or NIST CSF at a minimum and must be able to demonstrate compliance
on request.

**6. Staff security training**
The vendor must ensure that all staff with access to SecureSupply
systems or data complete security awareness training at least
annually.

**7. Access control**
The vendor must implement role-based access control ensuring that
only staff who require access to SecureSupply systems for their
job function are granted access.

**8. Breach notification — enhanced**
The vendor must notify SecureSupply Corp of any confirmed or
suspected data breach affecting SecureSupply data within 24 hours
of discovery. Notification must include a description of the
incident, the data affected, and the steps being taken to contain it.

**9. Security addendum**
The vendor must sign SecureSupply Corp's standard security addendum
which supplements the contract with specific technical and
organisational security requirements.

**10. Subcontractor restrictions**
The vendor must not subcontract any work involving SecureSupply
data to a third party without prior written consent. Any approved
subcontractors must meet the same security standards as the vendor.

---

## Additional Requirements for Tier 1 Vendors

In addition to all Tier 2 requirements above, all Tier 1 vendor
contracts must include:

**11. Certification requirement**
The vendor must maintain ISO 27001 certification or a current
SOC 2 Type II report covering the services provided to SecureSupply
Corp. Evidence must be provided annually.

**12. Penetration testing**
The vendor must conduct an independent penetration test of all
systems used to provide services to SecureSupply Corp at least
annually and must provide the executive summary to SecureSupply
Corp within 30 days of test completion.

**13. Right to audit**
SecureSupply Corp reserves the right to conduct or commission
an independent security audit of the vendor's systems and processes
with 30 days written notice. The vendor must cooperate fully with
such audits.

**14. Enhanced breach notification**
The vendor must notify SecureSupply Corp of any confirmed or
suspected security incident affecting SecureSupply data within
4 hours of discovery. A full incident report must be provided
within 5 business days.

**15. Business continuity and disaster recovery**
The vendor must maintain a Business Continuity Plan and Disaster
Recovery Plan and must test recovery capabilities at least annually.
Evidence of the most recent test must be provided to SecureSupply
Corp on request.

**16. Data residency**
Client financial data must only be stored and processed in
jurisdictions approved in writing by SecureSupply Corp. The vendor
must notify SecureSupply Corp at least 60 days before making any
changes to data residency.

**17. Encryption requirements**
All SecureSupply data must be encrypted at rest using AES-256 or
equivalent and in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher. Encryption keys
must be managed separately from the encrypted data.

**18. Background checks**
The vendor must conduct background checks on all staff who have
access to SecureSupply client data before granting access.
Background checks must be renewed every three years.

**19. Financial stability**
The vendor must notify SecureSupply Corp immediately if they
enter administration, receivership, or any insolvency proceedings
so that SecureSupply can activate its continuity arrangements.

## Contract Review Checklist

Before any vendor contract is signed the Vendor Risk Manager
must confirm that all required clauses are present:

**Tier 3 checklist:**
- [ ] Clause 1 — Compliance with laws
- [ ] Clause 2 — Basic incident notification
- [ ] Clause 3 — Confidentiality
- [ ] Clause 4 — Data return on termination

**Tier 2 checklist (in addition to Tier 3):**
- [ ] Clause 5 — Security standards compliance
- [ ] Clause 6 — Staff security training
- [ ] Clause 7 — Access control
- [ ] Clause 8 — Enhanced breach notification
- [ ] Clause 9 — Security addendum signed
- [ ] Clause 10 — Subcontractor restrictions

**Tier 1 checklist (in addition to Tier 2):**

- [ ] Clause 13 — Right to audit

- [ ] Clause 15 — BCP and DR requirements
